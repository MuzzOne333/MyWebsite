The body conducts electricity like a wire

When lightning hits, the current often travels over the skin in a phenomenon called a flashover. This can cause burns, blow off clothing, and ignite hair without always penetrating deep into the body.

The nervous system gets scrambled

Lightning can instantly stop the heart or breathing by disrupting the brain’s and spinal cord’s electrical signals. Survivors often report memory loss, confusion, or long-term neurological issues.

Unique skin markings can appear

Some people develop Lichtenberg figures — fern-like, reddish patterns on the skin caused by ruptured capillaries. They’re temporary but very distinctive to lightning strikes.
